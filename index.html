Popular Beliefs

90% of People Graduate College

Actually, only 64% of students graduate within 6 years in the U.S.

Most People Live to 100 Years

The average life expectancy is around 77 years in the U.S.

85% of Graduates Get a Job Immediately

Only about 53% secure a job within 6 months after graduating.

You Need to Drink 8 Glasses of Water Daily

There’s no fixed amount; hydration needs vary by individual.

Shaving Makes Hair Grow Thicker

Shaving doesn't affect the thickness or rate of hair growth.

Humans Only Use 10% of Their Brain

Humans use virtually all parts of their brain throughout the day.

Sugar Causes Hyperactivity in Kids

Studies show no strong evidence linking sugar to hyperactivity.

Lightning Never Strikes the Same Place Twice

Lightning can and often does strike the same place multiple times.

Goldfish Have a 3-Second Memory

Goldfish have memories that last for months.

Cracking Knuckles Causes Arthritis

There’s no evidence linking knuckle-cracking to arthritis.

Dogs Sweat Through Their Tongues

Dogs primarily regulate their temperature by panting, not sweating.

Eating Carrots Improves Your Vision

While carrots are good for your eyes, they don't improve vision significantly.

Cold Weather Causes Colds

Colds are caused by viruses, not cold temperatures.

Humans Swallow an Average of 8 Spiders a Year

This is a myth; it is highly unlikely that anyone swallows spiders while sleeping.

Alcohol Warms You Up

Alcohol causes blood vessels to dilate, leading to heat loss.

Fortune Cookies Are a Chinese Tradition

Fortune cookies originated in the U.S., not China.

Reading in Dim Light Harms Your Eyes

It may cause eye strain, but it doesn't cause permanent damage.

All Crystals Are Healing

While crystals can have aesthetic appeal, there is no scientific evidence for healing properties.

Bananas Grow on Trees

Bananas grow on large herbaceous plants, not trees.

Horses Can Sleep Standing Up

Horses can rest while standing, but they need to lie down to get REM sleep.

Touching Baby Birds Will Make Their Mothers Reject Them

Most birds have a limited sense of smell and won't reject their young.

There Are 7 Colors in a Rainbow

Rainbows are a spectrum of colors; some can see more than seven colors.

Peanuts Are Nuts

Peanuts are legumes, not true nuts.

More People Die in Plane Crashes than Car Accidents

Car accidents are far more common and deadly than plane crashes.

Wearing a Hat Causes Hair Loss

Wearing hats does not cause baldness or hair loss.

Touching a Toad Causes Warts

Warts are caused by a virus, not by touching toads.

Chameleons Change Color to Blend In

Chameleons change color for communication and temperature regulation, not just camouflage.

Gum Takes 7 Years to Digest

Gum passes through the digestive system in the same way as other foods.

Eating Chocolate Causes Acne

There’s no direct evidence linking chocolate consumption to acne.

Myth of the 5-Second Rule

Bacteria can transfer to food almost instantly, regardless of how long it has been on the floor.

You Should Wait an Hour After Eating to Swim

There is no evidence to support this; swimming after eating is safe.

Vegetarians Don't Get Enough Protein

With proper planning, vegetarians can get all the protein they need.

Electric Eels Are Eels

Electric eels are actually a type of knifefish, not true eels.

All Swans Are Monogamous

While many swans are monogamous, some may switch partners.

Fish Have Short Memories

Fish can have memories lasting weeks to months.

Snakes Can Hypnotize Their Prey

Snakes do not hypnotize; they rely on stealth and ambush.

Humans Have the Most Advanced Brain

While human brains are complex, other species exhibit unique intelligence.

Black Cats Bring Bad Luck

In many cultures, black cats are seen as symbols of good luck.

Frogs Can Jump Out of Boiling Water

Frogs may stay in hot water until it's too late; it's a myth.

People Swallow a Lot of Hair

It’s unlikely that anyone swallows enough hair to cause harm.

Pigeons Are Dirty Birds

Pigeons can be clean, and many are raised as pets.

Spicy Food Causes Ulcers

Spicy food doesn’t cause ulcers; bacteria do.

All Bacteria Are Harmful

Many bacteria are beneficial and essential for health.

Touching a Frog Gives You Warts

Warts are caused by a virus, not frogs.

More Taste Buds Mean More Flavor

The number of taste buds varies by person, but does not necessarily equate to flavor sensitivity.

Muscles Turn to Fat if You Stop Exercising

Muscle tissue cannot turn into fat; they are different types of tissue.

You Can't See the Great Wall of China from Space

The Great Wall is not visible from low Earth orbit without aid.

Drinking Milk Causes Mucus Production

There is no scientific evidence linking milk consumption to increased mucus production.

Goldfish Can Live in Bowls Forever

Goldfish require adequate space and care to thrive; bowls are often inadequate.

Cell Phones Cause Cancer

Current research does not conclusively link cell phone use to cancer.

Starfish Have No Brains

Starfish have a decentralized nerve net and can respond to stimuli.

Blue Whales Are the Largest Animals Ever

Blue whales are indeed the largest animals ever, surpassing even the largest dinosaurs.

Hummingbirds Can't Walk

Hummingbirds can walk, but they prefer to fly.

Cats Always Land on Their Feet

Cats usually land on their feet but can be injured in falls from great heights.

Vikings Wore Horned Helmets

There is no evidence that Vikings wore horned helmets; this is a popular myth.
